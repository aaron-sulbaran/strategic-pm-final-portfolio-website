# Internal Note: Response to Customer Letter

**From:** Greg Joswiak, SVP of Worldwide Marketing
**To:** Apple Wallet Product Team

---

Team,

I'm sharing a letter we received from a user in New York that I think captures exactly what we set out to accomplish. Marcus isn't a power user writing in to request features. He's someone who stopped carrying a physical wallet four months ago and only just noticed. That's the signal we should be paying attention to.

His experience validates the strategic direction we've been building toward: Apple Wallet is no longer just a payment tool. It's becoming the identity and access layer of the ecosystem. When a user's driver's license, transit credentials, office badge, payment methods, and travel documents all live in one place, and when those credentials surface contextually without the user having to think about it, we've moved past convenience into something closer to infrastructure.

His suggestion about healthcare is worth flagging specifically. We're already in conversations with insurers and provider networks about credential verification through Wallet, and what Marcus described is exactly the use case that will accelerate those partnerships. The broader opportunity here is clear: every category of physical credential that still requires a card, a form, or a manual lookup is a category where Wallet can expand. Healthcare, workplace verification, peer-to-peer identity sharing, and eventually credentials that don't even have a physical equivalent yet. Each one deepens the product's role in our users' lives and extends the partnership ecosystem that funds this platform.

From a business perspective, this is the kind of usage pattern that drives long-term ecosystem retention. Marcus isn't choosing Apple Wallet in isolation. He's choosing it because it's embedded in his iPhone, his Apple Watch, and the daily systems he depends on. That integration is what creates switching costs that no competitor can replicate with software alone. It also deepens our relationships with the partners who make this work: the banks, the transit authorities, the state agencies, and the employers issuing digital credentials through our platform. Every new credential type we support expands the surface area of the product and the value of those partnerships.

This is the kind of letter that reminds us why we build for the experience of the user. Keep going.
